How it works

Up and running in three steps.

No setup weekend. No consultant. You add it, it learns, it starts helping.

01

Add it to your group

Invite labor.fun into your Slack channel, Telegram group, Discord server, or Teams — the same way you'd add a person.

02

It learns your world

Point it at your docs and it picks up your people, your projects, and how your group actually works. Its memory is yours alone.

03

It does the work

Ask questions, hand off tasks, set up recurring reports. It answers in the chat and remembers what came before.

What it actually does

Real work, not a chatbot demo.

Concrete jobs your group needs done, handled in the chat you're already in.

Answers from your docs

Ask what your handbook, notes, or ledger actually says — it answers with the source, not a guess.

Research

Send it a question and it digs, reads, and comes back with a clear answer you can act on.

Drafting

Announcements, meeting recaps, grant blurbs, replies. It writes the first version so you don't stare at a blank page.

Scheduling & reminders

"Remind us Thursday," "nudge the group before the deadline." It keeps time so nobody has to.

Tasks & decisions

It tracks who owns what and what the group decided — so "wait, did we agree on this?" has an answer.

Recurring reports

Weekly agendas, finance summaries, status roundups — posted automatically, on the day you pick.

Why it's different

Built by a co-op. Answers to you, not to investors.

Most workplace software is built to watch your team and sell what it learns. This isn't that.

Private by design

Every group runs in its own isolated container with its own memory. Your conversations and files never touch another group's — and there are no third-party trackers.

Worker-owned, not VC-owned

labor.fun is built by a cooperative and paid for by the tools it makes — not venture capital, not ads, not selling your data. The incentives point at you.

Transparent by default

Open about how it works and what it does with your information. No black box, no fine print designed to trip you up.

Your team's tools should work for your team.

That's the whole idea. We build the assistant, you own how it's used, and the money comes from the product — not from surveilling the people using it.
